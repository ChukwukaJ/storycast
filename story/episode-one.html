Roots & Routes · Episode 01

The Sound of Home

Audio · 28 min · Published June 10, 2025

About This Episode

What does home sound like when you're 5,000 miles away? In this opening episode of Roots & Routes, we follow three members of the African diaspora — a Ghanaian musician in London, a Rwandan poet in Montreal, and a Nigerian writer in Lagos — as they reflect on the sounds that shaped their identity.

Produced by the StoryCast editorial team with field recordings, original compositions, and unscripted conversation. This episode contains some content in Twi, Kinyarwanda, and Yoruba with English translations provided in the transcript.

Full Transcript

[00:00] [Field recording: morning market sounds, overlapping voices, distant drumming]

Narrator: Sound is memory before it becomes language. For many in the African diaspora, the sounds of home — market chatter, rain on zinc roofs, the particular hum of a city at dusk — carry a kind of grief that photographs cannot.

[02:15] Kwame (London): The first thing I looked for when I moved to London was somewhere that sounded right. Not looked right — sounded right. I found a market near Peckham. Fridays only. And for about twenty minutes, it was close enough.

[05:40] Amara (Montreal): [In Kinyarwanda, translated] "You carry the hills with you." That's what my grandmother told me. She didn't mean it metaphorically. She meant the sound of wind across the hills. I hear it sometimes in winter here — when the wind comes off the Saint Lawrence. Just for a second.

[11:00] Tolu (Lagos): I never left, so for me it's the opposite. I watch my cousins come back from abroad and I see them hear Lagos for the first time again. The generators, the traffic, the hawkers. Their faces change. Like they're remembering something they didn't know they'd forgotten.

[18:30] Narrator: What unites all three voices is not nostalgia — it's attention. A trained ear that knows what it's listening for, even when the sound isn't there.

[25:00] [Closing field recording: evening prayer call fading into city ambience]

[End of Episode 1. Next episode: Market Voices — Lagos, Nigeria.]
